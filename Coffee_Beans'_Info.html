Coffee Beans' Info

Arabica: The most popular 60-70% of production, usually grown at higher altitudes (950 to 2,000m). They offer a smooth, sweet, floral, or fruity taste, often used for specialty coffee.

Robusta: Contains more caffeine 2 to 3% and has a bolder, bitter, and earthy taste. These plants thrive at lower altitudes, are more disease-resistant, and are often used for instant coffee or espresso blends.

Liberica: Rare and largely found in Southeast Asia, with a smoky, floral, or woody, and sometimes fruity profile.

Excelsa: Complex, combining light and dark roast characteristics with a tart, fruity, and wine-like taste. Technically a part of the Liberica family but recognized for its distinct, intense, and complex profile and used to add depth and complexity to blends.
