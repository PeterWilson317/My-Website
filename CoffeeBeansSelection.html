Coffee Blends

Coffee Blends combine beans from different origins, varieties (Arabica/Robusta), or roast levels to create a balanced, consistent flavor profile often featuring notes like chocolate, caramel, or nuts.

Coffee Blends Available

Arabica + Robusta

Arabica and Robusta blends combine the sweet, acidic, and aromatic notes of Arabica with the bold, earthy, and caffeine-rich properties of Robusta.

Popular ratios include 70/30 or 80/20 (Arabica/Robusta) for balanced espresso, or 50/50 for a, strong, crema-rich Thai-style drink,.

These blends offer better, thicker crema for espresso and a stronger, more consistent body.

Regional Blends

Regional coffee bean blends combine beans from specific geographical areas - primarily Latin America, Africa, and Asia/Pacific - to create distinct, consistent flavor profiles.

These blends often balance high-acidity, fruity African beans with nutty, chocolatey South American beans to create a balanced, versatile cup that offers more stability than single-origin options.

House Blends

A house blend is a signature coffee mixture created by a roaster or café, combining beans from different origins to achieve a consistent, balanced, and unique flavor profile.

Designed to be a reliable, year-round "go-to" coffee, it is usually roasted to a medium level, offering a smooth, crowd-pleasing taste popular in cafes.

Roasted Blends

Coffee roast blends are combinations of different coffee beans, often from various origins or roasted to different levels, designed to create a balanced, consistent, and complex flavor profile.

They are produced by blending before roasting (pre-blend) or after (post-roast), blending to combine the acidity of light roasts with the body of dark roasts.

Single-Origin Coffee

Single-origin coffee refers to beans sourced from one specific geographic location, such as a single farm, cooperative, or region within a country. Unlike blends, these coffees offer a pure, traceable flavor profile that reflects their unique terroir, often highlighting distinct fruity, floral, or nutty notes that are best enjoyed black or through light roasts.
