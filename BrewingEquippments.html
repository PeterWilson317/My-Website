Brewing Equipments

Here are the Equipments for Coffees

V60
$30

V60 is a iconic 60-degree, cone-shaped pour-over coffee dripper featuring spiral ribs and a large, single hole for precise, fast extraction that produces a clean, nuanced cup.

Turkish Coffee Pot
$45

Turkish coffee pot, known as a cezve (jezz-veh) or ibrik, is a small, long-handled vessel with a narrow neck and wider base designed specifically for brewing Turkish coffee.

French Press Coffee Maker
$40

French press is a simple, manual coffee maker consisting of a cylindrical beaker, plunger, and mesh filter that brews rich, full-bodied coffee by steeping coarse grounds in hot water for four minutes before filtering. It is known for producing a robust, oil-rich cup, using a 1:15 ratio, and requiring no paper filters.

Moka Pot
$50

Moka pot is a stovetop coffee maker that brews rich, intense coffee similar to espresso by passing boiling water pressurized by steam 1 - 2 bars through ground coffee.

Aero Press
$40

AeroPress is a versatile, portable coffee maker that uses a 3-in-1 brewing method (immersion, aeration, and pressure) to create smooth, rich, grit-free coffee in about a minute.

Chemex
$50

Chemex coffeemaker consists of an hourglass-shaped glass flask with a conical funnel-like neck and proprietary filters, made of bonded paper, that are thicker than the standard paper filters used for a drip coffeemaker.

Vietnamese Drip
$25

Vietnamese drip coffee maker, or Phin, is a small, four-piece stainless steel or aluminum brewing tool. It sits directly on top of a mug and uses gravity to slowly brew a strong, dark, single-cup coffee, traditionally served with sweetened condensed milk.

Cold Brew
$50

Cold brew coffee makers create smooth, low-acid coffee by steeping coarse grounds in cold water for 12–24 hours, often using immersion (filter baskets) or drip methods.
